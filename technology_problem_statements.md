## TWO-TECHNOLOGY COMBINATIONS

### 1. Finance + Cybersecurity

#### Problem Statement 1.1: Ransomware Escalation in Financial Institutions
Financial institutions experienced ransomware incidents totaling over $2.1 billion in payments between 2022 and 2024, with 2023 reaching the highest level at $1.1 billion. Cybercriminals are using more sophisticated methods including advanced persistent threats and leveraging AI/ML to exploit vulnerabilities, creating urgent needs for enhanced defense mechanisms.

**Reference:** FinCEN (2025). Financial Trend Analysis on Ransomware. https://www.fincen.gov/news/news-releases/fincen-issues-financial-trend-analysis-ransomware

#### Problem Statement 1.2: Third-Party Vendor Security Risks
Third-party data breaches continue to pose major threats to the financial sector, requiring careful vendor vetting and monitoring to ensure rigorous cybersecurity standards. Financial institutions' increasing dependence on cloud computing and payment processing vendors creates significant attack surfaces.

**Reference:** First Bank & Trust Company (2025). Cybersecurity in 2025: What Financial Institutions Need to Know. https://www.firstbank.com/resources/learning-center/cybersecurity-in-2025-what-financial-institutions-need-to-know/

#### Problem Statement 1.3: Operational Resilience and DDoS Attacks
Disruptive and destructive cyberattacks, such as ransomware and distributed denial of service attacks, continue to compromise security of technology systems, affect operations, and result in breaches of sensitive information across all sectors, including banking.

**Reference:** OCC (2025). Cybersecurity and Financial System Resilience Report 2025. https://www.occ.gov/publications-and-resources/publications/cybersecurity-and-financial-system-resilience/files/pub-2025-cybersecurity-report.pdf

#### Problem Statement 1.4: Cloud Security Vulnerabilities
Financial institutions moving to hybrid or cloud-based infrastructures face new attack vectors, with cybercriminals increasingly targeting vulnerabilities in cloud storage and services. Organizations need strict access controls, encryption, and regular audits to protect against data breaches.

**Reference:** First Bank & Trust Company (2025). Cybersecurity in 2025: What Financial Institutions Need to Know. https://www.firstbank.com/resources/learning-center/cybersecurity-in-2025-what-financial-institutions-need-to-know/

#### Problem Statement 1.5: Budget Constraints vs. Evolving Threats
Banks face limited budgets to dedicate to cybersecurity measures, while budgets are not growing in proportion to increasing demands. This creates resource allocation challenges when attempting to protect against sophisticated cyber threats.

**References:** 
- DashDevs (2024). Banking Cybersecurity Challenges: Threats & Solutions for 2025. https://dashdevs.com/blog/cybersecurity-in-banking-main-threats-and-challenges-in-2023/
- KPMG (2025). Cybersecurity Considerations 2025: Financial Services. https://kpmg.com/xx/en/our-insights/ai-and-technology/cybersecurity-considerations-2025/financial-services.html

### 10. Blockchain + Quantum Computing

#### Problem Statement 10.1: Public Key Cryptography Vulnerability
Quantum algorithms, such as Shor's algorithm, can undermine public-key cryptography, while Grover's algorithm accelerates brute-force search, weakening proof-of-work schemes.

**Reference:** MDPI (2025). Quantum Blockchain Framework. https://www.mdpi.com/2227-7072/13/4/220

#### Problem Statement 10.2: Blockchain Migration to Post-Quantum Cryptography
Post-quantum cryptography comes with tradeoffs: larger key sizes, higher computational costs, and increased transaction sizes that can affect throughput, fees, and user experience.

**Reference:** Blockchain Council (2025). Blockchain Preparing for Quantum Threats. https://www.blockchain-council.org/blockchain/blockchain-preparing-for-quantum-threats/

#### Problem Statement 10.3: Hard Fork Coordination Challenges
Transition protocols require hard forks of blockchains, meaning two blockchains running in parallel, with the challenge of convincing the majority of peers to join the new blockchain.

**Reference:** Frontiers (2025). Novel Transition Protocol to Post-Quantum Cryptocurrency. https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/computer-science/articles/10.3389/fcomp.2025.1457000/full

#### Problem Statement 10.4: Legacy UTXO Vulnerability
Bitcoin's underperformance reflects the creeping weight of existential risk from quantum computing, with institutions reassessing security and the network's ability to upgrade. Old unspent transaction outputs remain vulnerable to quantum attacks.

**Reference:** Yahoo Finance (2026). Quantum Computing Already Hitting Bitcoin. https://finance.yahoo.com/news/quantum-computing-already-hitting-bitcoin-150000966.html

#### Problem Statement 10.5: Quantum-Resistant Consensus Mechanisms
Existing consensus algorithms (PoW, PoS) need redesign to remain secure against quantum attacks while maintaining decentralization and performance.

**Reference:** Nature (2025). Quantum Secured Blockchain Framework. https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-025-16315-8
